## Full Bio (for website, LinkedIn, and speaker kit)

Naval Thakur is an enterprise cloud and DevOps leader with 18+ years of hands-on experience transforming engineering organisations at scale. As Practice Manager at SLB, one of the world's largest energy technology companies, he has led DevSecOps, FinOps, and GenAI adoption programmes spanning global delivery teams, saving over $600K in cloud costs and accelerating delivery cycles across dozens of projects.

Naval is the creator of the **CognitiveOps Maturity Model** — a four-layer framework that maps an organisation's journey from manual, reactive operations (Layer 1) through automation and intelligent tooling, to AI-autonomous engineering at scale (Layer 4). The model is freely available at nthakur.com and has been used to assess and guide dozens of engineering teams.

He holds 39+ professional certifications — including FinOps Certified Practitioner, Google Cloud GenAI Leader, Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA), AWS Solutions Architect, and TOGAF 9. He is an MBA Gold Medalist from MDI Gurgaon. Naval speaks, trains, and writes at the intersection of cloud operations, financial accountability, and AI adoption — always practitioner-first.

### 1. From DevOps to CognitiveOps: The Next Five Years

**Tags:** DevOps, AI, Future of Engineering, Leadership  
**Format:** Keynote or conference talk (30–45 min)  
**Audience:** Engineering leaders, architects, CTOs, DevOps practitioners

**Abstract:**  
Most organisations are still celebrating the fact that they have a CI/CD pipeline. But the frontier has already moved. In this talk, Naval maps the next five years of enterprise engineering — from the automation we have now to the AI-orchestrated, self-healing, policy-driven systems that forward-thinking organisations are already piloting. Drawing on the CognitiveOps Maturity Model — a four-layer framework developed from 18+ years of enterprise transformation — Naval shows audiences exactly where they sit today, what Layer 4 looks like in practice, and the specific decisions that separate organisations that will lead this transition from those that will follow. Practitioner-first, no vendor slides, and genuinely actionable.

**Key takeaways:**
- The four CognitiveOps maturity layers and how to self-assess your organisation in under 10 minutes
- The three transitions that matter most: from reactive to proactive, from automated to intelligent, from human-approved to policy-governed
- What the earliest Layer 4 organisations are actually doing differently today

### 2. GenAI in Enterprise Operations: Patterns That Actually Work

**Tags:** GenAI, AIOps, Enterprise AI, LLMOps, Productivity  
**Format:** Conference talk or workshop (45–60 min)  
**Audience:** Engineering managers, SREs, platform engineers, AI leads

**Abstract:**  
The enterprise GenAI conversation has been dominated by demos, proofs-of-concept, and vendor promises. This talk cuts through it. Naval shares the patterns that have actually delivered measurable ROI in large engineering organisations — and the patterns that look great in a pilot but fail at scale. Topics covered include: how to operationalise LLMs without introducing unacceptable latency or cost, the governance structures that prevent AI from creating compliance risk, and how to measure GenAI return on investment in a way that finance and leadership will accept. Grounded in real examples from production deployments, not research papers.

**Key takeaways:**
- The three GenAI adoption anti-patterns that kill enterprise programmes
- A practical governance framework: approved use cases, data classification, output auditing, cost guardrails
- How to build an ROI model that connects AI spend to business outcomes your CFO will recognise

### 3. Cloud Financial Intelligence: What FinOps Practitioners Get Wrong

**Tags:** FinOps, Cloud Cost, Cloud Financial Management, FinOps Foundation  
**Format:** Conference talk (30–45 min)  
**Audience:** FinOps practitioners, engineering managers, finance leads, CTOs

**Abstract:**  
Naval has been running FinOps programmes in enterprise cloud environments for years. In that time, he has seen the same mistakes repeated at organisations of every size and cloud maturity. This talk is a practitioner's honest post-mortem: the tagging strategies that seemed sensible but created unmanageable overhead, the chargeback models that caused engineering–finance conflict rather than eliminating it, and the forecasting approaches that were too simplistic to survive a product launch. Naval shares what he has learned from the failures alongside the patterns that actually moved the needle — and he names the things the FinOps Foundation curriculum doesn't always tell you.

**Key takeaways:**
- The single most common reason FinOps programmes stall after the first quarter
- The difference between showback that engages engineers and showback that gets ignored
- How to build a cost forecasting model that survives real-world demand spikes

### 4. Achieving Enterprise-wide Agility: Beyond the Technology Stack

**Tags:** Agile, Transformation, Culture, Leadership, Change Management  
**Format:** Keynote or conference talk (45–60 min)  
**Audience:** Engineering directors, transformation leads, HR/organisational design, senior leadership

**Abstract:**  
Most enterprise agile transformations fail not because the teams lack the skills, but because the organisation's structure, incentives, and culture contradict agile principles at every level above the team. Naval has led and participated in agile transformations at scale — and this talk is the honest debrief. He maps the specific structural and cultural blockers that prevent enterprise agility from spreading beyond the first few pilot teams, and the interventions that have actually worked. Part organisational design, part change management, part practitioner confession.

**Key takeaways:**
- Why agile at team level almost never translates to agility at portfolio level without deliberate structural changes
- The three cultural patterns that kill enterprise agile transformations — and how to identify them early
- A practical roadmap for leaders who want to move from "doing agile" to being an agile organisation

### 5. Infrastructure as Code with Azure Pipelines: Terraform + SaltStack in Production

**Tags:** IaC, Terraform, Azure Pipelines, DevOps, Cloud Infrastructure  
**Format:** Technical conference talk or workshop (45–75 min)  
**Audience:** DevOps engineers, platform engineers, cloud architects, SREs

**Abstract:**  
Infrastructure as Code sounds straightforward in tutorials. It is considerably messier in large-scale enterprise production environments. In this session, Naval shares hard-won lessons from running Terraform and SaltStack deployments through Azure Pipelines at enterprise scale — covering the drift detection strategies that actually work, the state management approaches that don't create operational risk, and the pipeline design decisions that separate maintainable IaC from a slowly accumulating mess. Includes live demonstrations and practical patterns you can apply immediately.

**Key takeaways:**
- How to structure Terraform modules for enterprise-scale reuse without over-engineering
- State management strategies for large multi-team environments
- How to build drift detection into your pipeline before it becomes an audit finding

### 6. The 7 Cs of DevOps: A Framework That Actually Sticks

**Tags:** DevOps, Continuous Delivery, CI/CD, Platform Engineering, Culture  
**Format:** Conference talk or workshop (45–60 min)  
**Audience:** DevOps practitioners, engineering managers, developers, SREs

**Abstract:**  
Every DevOps framework has a list of practices — and most teams can recite them. Fewer teams have actually connected those practices into a coherent, measurable system. Naval's 7 Cs framework — Continuous Integration, Continuous Delivery, Continuous Deployment, Continuous Quality, Continuous Compliance, Continuous Monitoring, and Continuous Feedback — gives engineering teams a structured lens for diagnosing gaps and sequencing improvements. This talk introduces the framework and walks through how to use it to identify your single highest-leverage next step, regardless of where your team currently sits on the maturity curve.

**Key takeaways:**
- How to use the 7 Cs as a diagnostic to find your team's actual constraint (not the one you think it is)
- The order in which the 7 Cs deliver value — and why skipping steps is expensive
- Practical metrics for each C that you can start tracking this week

### 7. Seven Habits of Highly Effective DevOps Teams

**Tags:** DevOps, Culture, Team Effectiveness, Engineering Excellence  
**Format:** Conference talk (30–45 min)  
**Audience:** Engineering managers, DevOps practitioners, team leads, developers

**Abstract:**  
Not all DevOps teams are equally effective — and the difference is rarely the tools they use. Naval has worked with dozens of engineering teams across industries and geographies. In this talk, he distils the seven habits that consistently separate high-performing DevOps teams from teams that are perpetually firefighting. These are behavioural and cultural habits, not tooling choices — which means they are available to every team regardless of stack or budget. Practically framed, with real examples and the anti-patterns that signal each habit is missing.

**Key takeaways:**
- The single habit that has the highest correlation with fast mean-time-to-recovery
- How high-performing teams handle blameless culture in practice (not just in theory)
- The two habits most commonly missing in otherwise technically mature teams

### 8. Building Trust Economies: Blockchain, Smart Contracts & DApps on Azure

**Tags:** Blockchain, Smart Contracts, DApps, Azure, Web3  
**Format:** Conference talk (45–60 min)  
**Audience:** Architects, developers, enterprise innovation leads, technology strategists

**Abstract:**  
Blockchain's moment of peak hype has passed — which makes it an ideal time to have an honest conversation about where distributed ledger technology actually delivers enterprise value. Naval built and presented blockchain solutions on Azure at multiple public conferences, including Global Azure Bootcamp and APGI. This talk covers the architectures that have worked in real-world enterprise deployments: the governance models for smart contract lifecycle management, the trust assumptions that DApps actually require, and the specific use cases where blockchain's tamper-evidence properties are genuinely valuable versus where a traditional database would serve better.

**Key takeaways:**
- The three enterprise use cases where blockchain's properties justify the complexity
- How to design smart contract governance for a corporate environment (spoiler: it is not the same as a public chain)
- Lessons from deploying DApps in Azure — what the platform handles well and where you are on your own
